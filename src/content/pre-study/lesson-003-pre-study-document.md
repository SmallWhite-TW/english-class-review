# English Course Pre-study Document (Lesson 003)

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Topic:** Taiwan Economic Trends, Diplomacy & Aviation News

---

## 1-Minute Quick Review (上課前 1 分鐘速讀)

- **Economy:** Taiwan's industrial output hit a record high in February, mainly because AI and semiconductor demand stayed strong.
- **Diplomacy:** Taiwan protested after Denmark labeled some Taiwanese nationals as "Chinese" on residency permits.
- **Aviation news:** Airlines may reduce some Asia flights if fuel supplies remain uncertain.
- **Accident topic:** The Air Canada runway accident shows the risks of ground operations even after landing.

---

## Part 1: Business & Economy

**Article:** _Taiwan's industrial, manufacturing output hits record high in February_

### 1. Key Vocabulary (關鍵字彙)

- **Industrial output** (n.) 工業產值
- **Manufacturing activity** (n.) 製造業活動
- **Record high** (n. phr.) 歷史新高
- **Year-on-year (YoY)** (adj./adv.) 與去年同期相比
- **Surging** (adj.) 激增的、飆升的
- **Infrastructure** (n.) 基礎設施
- **High-performance computing (HPC)** (n.) 高效能運算
- **Semiconductor** (n.) 半導體
- **Hampered** (v.) 阻礙、妨礙

### 2. Article Summary (文章要點)

- **Growth driver:** Taiwan's industrial and manufacturing output reached a record high in February, mainly driven by strong global demand for AI-related products.
- **Key sectors:** Electronic components, integrated circuits, and optical products performed especially well.
- **Different situation across industries:** While AI and semiconductor-related sectors grew strongly, some traditional industries remained weaker, showing an uneven recovery.

### 3. Short Sharing Version (可直接分享的英文短講)

_One article I read is about Taiwan's industrial and manufacturing output reaching a record high in February. The main reason was strong global demand for AI and semiconductor-related products. I think this shows Taiwan is very competitive in technology, but it also means the economy may depend too much on a few key industries._

## Part 2: International Relations & Identity

**Video:** _Foreign Ministry confronts Denmark for labeling Taiwanese nationals as Chinese_

### 1. Key Vocabulary (關鍵字彙)

- **Confront** (v.) 正面交涉、對質
- **Label** (v.) 標示、貼上分類
- **National** (n.) 國民、公民
- **Residency permit** (n. phr.) 居留證
- **Discriminatory** (adj.) 歧視性的
- **Diplomatic channel** (n. phr.) 外交管道
- **Identity** (n.) 身分認同
- **Respect** (v.) 尊重

### 2. Article Summary (文章要點)

- **Main issue:** Denmark reportedly labeled some Taiwanese nationals as "Chinese" on residency permits, which triggered a response from Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
- **Taiwan's reaction:** MOFA publicly objected to the wording and said it would continue communicating through diplomatic channels.
- **Why it matters:** The dispute is not only administrative. It also concerns national identity, dignity, and how Taiwanese people are represented overseas.

## Part 3: Airline Operations & Fuel Supply

**Video:** _Airlines consider cutting flights to Asia amid uncertain fuel supplies_

### 1. Key Vocabulary (關鍵字彙)

- **Fuel supply** (n. phr.) 燃料供應
- **Uncertain** (adj.) 不確定的
- **Consider** (v.) 考慮
- **Cut flights** (v. phr.) 減少航班
- **Route planning** (n. phr.) 航線規劃
- **Operational cost** (n. phr.) 營運成本
- **Disruption** (n.) 中斷、擾亂
- **Frequency** (n.) 班次頻率

### 2. Article Summary (文章要點)

- **Current concern:** Some airlines are worried that unstable fuel supplies in parts of Asia may affect normal flight operations.
- **Possible response:** Carriers may reduce flight frequency or adjust route planning to lower risk and control costs.
- **Broader impact:** Fuel uncertainty can influence ticket prices, travel convenience, cargo logistics, and airline scheduling decisions.

## Part 4: Aviation Safety & Accident Response

**Article:** _The Air Canada Accident at LaGuardia Airport_

### 1. Key Vocabulary (關鍵字彙)

- **Collision** (n.) 撞擊、碰撞
- **Runway** (n.) 跑道
- **Rescue vehicle** (n. phr.) 救援車輛
- **Firefighting vehicle** (n. phr.) 消防車輛
- **Emergency response** (n. phr.) 緊急應變
- **Investigation** (n.) 調查
- **Damage** (n.) 損害
- **After landing** (adv. phr.) 降落後

### 2. Article Summary (文章要點)

- **What happened:** An Air Canada jet collided with a Port Authority rescue or firefighting vehicle on the runway at LaGuardia Airport after landing.
- **Immediate aftermath:** Emergency vehicles gathered at the scene, and the damaged aircraft remained on the runway while authorities handled the situation.
- **Why this is important:** The accident highlights how dangerous ground operations can be, even after a plane has landed safely. It also raises questions about runway coordination and airport safety procedures.

### 3. Useful Sentence Patterns (實用句型)

- **The incident highlights...**
  - _The incident highlights the importance of runway safety._
- **Authorities are investigating whether...**
  - _Authorities are investigating whether there was a communication problem._
- **Even though..., ... still...**
  - _Even though the plane had landed, a serious accident still happened on the runway._

## Part 5: Famous Air Disaster Case Sharing (著名空難案例分享)

### 1. Recommended Case: Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 (建議分享案例)

- **What happened:** Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 disappeared in 2014 while flying from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing.
- **Why it became famous:** The plane vanished without a clear explanation, and the case became one of the world's most well-known aviation mysteries.
- **Why it is easy to discuss:** Even if you do not know many technical details, you can still talk about **disappearance**, **search efforts**, **public attention**, and **aviation safety**.

### 2. Short Sharing Version (可直接分享的英文短講)

_One famous air disaster I have heard about is Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370. The flight disappeared in 2014 on its way from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing. It became famous because the aircraft vanished and many questions were never fully answered. I think this case shocked people around the world and made them realize how important tracking systems and international search cooperation are._

### 3. My Opinion Example (個人看法範例)

_What impressed me most is that even modern aircraft can still disappear in unusual situations. This case shows that aviation safety is not only about flying, but also about communication, technology, and emergency response._

### 4. Useful Vocabulary (延伸字彙)

- **Human error** (n. phr.) 人為失誤
- **Mechanical failure** (n. phr.) 機械故障
- **Miscommunication** (n.) 溝通失誤
- **Black box** (n. phr.) 黑盒子，飛航紀錄器
- **Disappear** (v.) 消失
- **Search effort** (n. phr.) 搜尋行動
- **Safety regulation** (n. phr.) 安全規範
- **Crash investigation** (n. phr.) 空難調查
- **Death toll** (n. phr.) 死亡人數
- **Aviation lesson** (n. phr.) 航空安全教訓

### 5. Useful Sentence Patterns (實用句型)

- _One famous air disaster I have heard about is..._
- _This accident is remembered because..._
- _A key lesson from this case is that..._
- _It shows how important communication and procedure are in aviation._

### 6. Backup Cases (備用案例)

- **Tenerife airport disaster (1977):** good for discussing miscommunication.
- **Japan Airlines Flight 123 (1985):** good for discussing tragedy and safety awareness.
- **Air France Flight 447 (2009):** good for discussing automation and pilot response.

### 7. Movie Adaptation Case (有翻拍電影的案例)

**Case:** _US Airways Flight 1549 (2009)_

- **What happened:** Shortly after takeoff from New York, the plane hit a flock of birds and lost power in both engines. The captain successfully landed the aircraft on the Hudson River.
- **Why it became famous:** All 155 people on board survived, so the case is often remembered as a miracle landing and a strong example of calm decision-making under pressure.
- **Movie connection:** This case was adapted into the American film _Sully_.

**Short sharing version:**

_Another famous aviation accident I know is US Airways Flight 1549. After the plane hit birds, both engines stopped working. The captain landed the plane safely on the Hudson River, and everyone survived. This case became very famous and was later made into the movie Sully._

## Part 6: Discussion Questions (討論問題)

1. Do you think Taiwan's strong performance in AI and semiconductors is a long-term advantage or a potential risk?
2. Why do you think national identity is such a sensitive issue in international documents?
3. If airlines cut flights to Asia, what effects could that have on travelers and businesses?
4. In your opinion, what are the biggest risks during airport ground operations?
5. How should airports improve communication between aircraft crews and emergency vehicles?
6. Which famous air disaster have you heard about before, and what do you remember about it?
7. Which of these topics would be the easiest for you to discuss in English? Why?

---

## Part 7: Learning Goals (預習目標)

1. **Vocabulary:** Be able to explain the meanings of **industrial output**, **semiconductor**, **residency permit**, **fuel supply**, **collision**, and **runway** in English.
2. **Speaking:** Practice giving a short opinion using:
   - _I think this issue matters because..._
   - _One possible impact is..._
   - _Authorities should..._
3. **Discussion readiness:** Prepare to compare an economic topic, a political news topic, an aviation operations topic, an accident report, and one famous air disaster case in one lesson.
4. **Pronunciation:** Practice saying **discriminatory**, **operational**, **investigation**, **coordination**, and **semiconductor** clearly.
